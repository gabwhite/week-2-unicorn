The concept of Cyberfeminism defies a strict definition, and most feminist scholars would define Cyberfeminism as a range of debates, theories, and practices. Cyberfeminism is not a subject, but an approach to a subject such as gender stereotypes in cyberspace and gender equality in cyberspace.

What is the Unicorn Law?

Simply put The Unicorn Law suggests that women working in the tech industry are so rare, they're pretty much unicorns. Therefore, going to see a woman speak at a text conference is like seeing a real, live unicorn.

Basically, the Unicorn Law suggests that because women are tokenized in the tech industry as "Women in the Tech Industry" they're more likely to gain recognition for being a woman rather than the actual work they're producing.

This then becomes a double-edged sword: if a woman is invited to a tech conference to speak about being a woman in the tech industry, should she accept the invitation? On the one hand, it's a great opportunity for publicity and networking, but on the other hand she is still enforcing that as a woman in tech, you must talk about being a woman in tech.
